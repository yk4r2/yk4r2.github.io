Therefore, as we want all three terms to be equal, they all should have probability $\dfrac{1}{3}$, as they sum to $1$. In other words, we need to find a value $p$ such that the probability both of them obtain their first heads at the same time is $\dfrac{1}{3}$. The probability it takes exactly $k$ flips for each of them would be $p(1-p)^{k-1}$, as they get $k-1$ tails and $1$ head. Thus, the probability both get it in exactly $k$ flips is $p^2(1-p)^{2(k-1)}$. Summing this up from $k = 1$ to $\infty$ yields $$\displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^{\infty}p^2(1-p)^{2(k-1)} = p^2\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\left((1-p)^2\right)^{k-1} = \dfrac{p^2}{1 - (1-p)^2}$$ Setting this expression above equal to $\dfrac{1}{3}$, $$\dfrac{p^2}{1-(1-p)^2} = \dfrac{1}{3}$$ The only solution in $(0,1)$ is $p = \dfrac{1}{2}$.
